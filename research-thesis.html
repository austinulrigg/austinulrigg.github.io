Undergraduate honors thesis · University of Washington

Graph Genera and Minors

My undergraduate honors thesis, supervised by François Clément, studies how graphs embed on surfaces — with an emphasis on orientable surfaces, rotation systems, and forbidden minors.

It develops the historical background, from the earliest appearances of the crossing number $\mathrm{cr}(G)$ through Heawood’s map-coloring conjecture to the invention of rotation systems for encoding cellular embeddings. It then describes and analyzes PAGE, the rotation-system genus algorithm developed jointly with Alexander Metzger, including examples where it determined genera that were previously unknown. Finally, it surveys the problem of determining the full set of forbidden toroidal minors, summarizes known results, and discusses open problems — such as the genus of the Balaban $(3,11)$-cage.
